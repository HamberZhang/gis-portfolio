Blue Flow

GEOG 572 Lab 2: Interactive Scroll Map

"Blue Flow" is a visual aesthetic inspired by the interplay of movement, light, and atmosphere found in three artworks: Van Gogh’s The Starry Night, Hokusai’s The Great Wave off Kanagawa, and Monet’s Morning on the Seine in the Mist.

Together, these works create a spectrum of blue—from the swirling nocturnal sky, to the bold, dynamic curves of ocean waves, to the quiet dissolving mist on Monet’s river.

In my map design, I translate this shared blue vocabulary into cartographic form. Roads and land contours borrow rhythmic, flowing lines from Van Gogh; water layers reinterpret Hokusai’s deep indigo and crisp highlights; and terrain and background textures adopt Monet’s soft gradients and gentle atmospheric diffusion.

Across layers, warm accent lights echo small moments of human presence within a predominantly cool palette. The resulting map aims to visualize calm movement—where light glides across the surface, boundaries soften, and every form flows harmoniously within a unified blue atmosphere.
