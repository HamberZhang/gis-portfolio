California's Electric Future

My project California's Electric Future visualizes the spatial distribution and accessibility of EV fast-charging stations along California's major highway network. Focusing on the five primary corridors—Interstate 5, 10, 80, US-101, and State Route 99—the visualization examines how charging stations cluster in urban regions and remain sparse across the Sierra Nevada, Mojave Desert, and northern forested areas.

Data were drawn from the U.S. Department of Energy's dataset and Caltrans State Highway Lines (projected to EPSG: 5070). A 20 km buffer was generated around each station to estimate service areas, and highway segments were categorized as covered or uncovered based on their intersection with these buffers.
